    What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

  • Values allow for better decision-making in programming. Programmers need to consider how their code may be used and understood by other people and through better decision-making they will be able to make sure their decisions are better for everyone as a whole rather than just themselves. Such as quality of code, that it’s written clean, and it’s been maintained well and up to a good standard.
  • Empathy allows programmers to understand themselves from other people's perspectives, understanding how they think as a whole to certain situations. This is crucial because without empathy there may be problems that arise due to things like miscommunication or misunderstanding how others think. This links to collaborations, when working in a team environment, you need to understand that not everyone thinks the same as you and you need to understand why or how some people may feel a certain type of way.
  • Self-awareness enables programmers to recognise their strengths and weaknesses. This is essential for oneself to improve and grow because recognising the things that you’re good at and not as good at will help you understand what things you need to work on more. By understanding the things that you need to work on, it allows yourself to understand yourself more and ‘chill’ in a way that you recognise the problems and that you can take it easy rather than finding out by doing more work and continuously struggling in a particular thing.
